The dsp64 method specifies the signal processing function your object defines along with its arguments. Your object\textquotesingle{}s dsp64 method will be called when the M\+SP signal compiler is building a sequence of operations (known as the D\+SP Chain) that will be performed on each set of audio samples. The operation sequence consists of a pointers to functions (called perform routines) followed by arguments to those functions. To add an entry to the D\+SP chain, your dsp64 method uses the \char`\"{}dsp\+\_\+add64\char`\"{} method of the D\+SP chain. The dsp\+\_\+add64 method is passed an a pointer to your object, a pointer to a perform64 routine that calculates the samples, an optional flag which may alter behavior, and a generic pointer which will be passed on to your perform routine.
